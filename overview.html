OVERVIEW

 

Content
The Biomaterials Workshop is an interdisciplinary educational initiative concerned with design features and properties evident in natural materials, as well as what occurs when living systems and fabricated ones are interfaced in biomedical materials. The major classes of biomolecules—lipids, proteins, carbohydrates, nucleic acids, and biominerals—are considered, as they occur as coatings, adhesives, fibers, and structural materials in natural systems such as cell and plant surfaces, shell and bone, marine organisms, spiders, and other living organisms. Ways in which hierarchically structured assemblies of nature inspire new technological developments are also addressed. The second part of the workshop focuses on materials used in medicine. Biological responses to materials, the design of new biocompatible and degradable materials for implants, devices, adhesives, wound healing, drug delivery, and imaging, and new cellular therapies pertinent to organ and tissue transplantation will be discussed. Along with the science of biomaterials, medical, social, ethical and policy issues are also considered.

Participants
Perspectives from the sciences—biology, biochemistry, chemistry, environmental science, neuroscience, and physics—from medicine, or from engineering disciplines such as materials science, biomedical, chemical, mechanical fields have proven valuable for gaining an understanding of the interdisciplinary field of biomaterials. A solid introductory knowledge of science is assumed for most sessions, however, persons with interest in ethics and policy hoping to strengthen their understanding of important scientific advances stirring current social debate are also encouraged to participate in sessions of interest.

Varied levels of participation are welcome. Students, postdocs, faculty and other members of the university community are welcome to attend particular sessions and formal seminars as their interests dictate.

Students may enroll in Chem 744 or Chem 812-2 for course credit. (Chem 744: 4 credits; Chem 812-2: 3 credits). Along with the expectation of significant participation in weekly discussions, and leadership of some of these, students write a review paper on a topic of their choosing. To approximate the publishing process, participants review each other’s manuscripts and are given the opportunity to revise and format theirs before publication on the workshop website. Authors of exceptional papers are encouraged to submit their reviews for publication in the literature. For some student participants, it may be appropriate to confer and collaborate with their advisors on this project with the goal of publication in mind.

Format
The workshop meets once a week for 2.5 hours and functions as a highly interactive study group. Students and faculty undertake careful reading of recent and seminal literature posted ahead of time on the workshop website. Weekly sessions consist of presentations by invited experts and lively discussion between workshop participants who bring the varied perspectives of their disciplines. Generous support from the Dean of Arts and Sciences for Spring 2002 and 2003, has made it possible to invite distinguished experts from outside the university to share their findings in a public seminar and thereafter, to participate in a discussion with workshop participants and others who might like to attend. The last meeting of the semester will feature highlights of students’ projects and a reception.
